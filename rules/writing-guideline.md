# Writing Guidelines: Crisp, Clear, No Fluff

When to use: anytime you write a document.

Before writing: Name the decision or action the document drives. If you can't, stop.

Be direct:
- Sentences under 30 words, one message each. Split at "and/and but."
- Replace adjectives with data. ❌ "much faster" → ✅ "latency dropped from 340ms to 40ms"
- Direct questions get four answers: yes, no, a number, or "I don't know — will follow up by [date]."

Time & context:
- Absolute dates only. ❌ "last week" → ✅ "Feb 12, 2026"
- Define every acronym on first use. Never assume shared context.

Eliminate weak language:
- Weasel words (hedge without adding info): might, could, often, significant, some, many, recently, soon, very, essentially
- Peacock words (superiority without data): cutting-edge, innovative, best-in-class, state-of-the-art
- Every claim backed by a number or cited source.

Structure:
- Active voice throughout. ❌ "was rolled back by" → ✅ "team rolled back"
- One argument per paragraph: topic sentence → evidence → link forward.
- The document answers one clearly statable question or drives one specific decision.

Test before sending:
- Can you state the document's purpose in one sentence?
- Does every claim have a number or source?
- Any relative time references or undefined acronyms?
- Any sentence over 30 words?
